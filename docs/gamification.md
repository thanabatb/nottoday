# Emotional Reset App — Gamification Logic

## Gamification Philosophy
Gamification should support emotional awareness and recovery.
It should never reward emotional chaos for its own sake.

The system should make users feel:
- encouraged
- curious about their own patterns
- proud of small progress

It should never make users feel:
- guilty for slipping
- punished for emotional difficulty
- pressured to "win" at emotions

## Core Gamification Objectives
1. Encourage repeat visits
2. Reinforce self-awareness habits
3. Highlight emotional recovery
4. Reward reflection and consistency
5. Create a sense of momentum without pressure

## Recommended Systems
### 1. Awareness Streak
Tracks how many consecutive days the user checks in.

This is the safest primary streak for MVP because it rewards awareness rather than emotional performance.

**Example logic:**
- user checks in at least once during the day → streak continues
- no check-in during the day → streak resets or soft-breaks depending on product strategy

### 2. Calm Day Indicator
This is a secondary signal, not the primary streak.

A calm day can mean:
- the user checked in and selected a low emotional intensity
- the user did not need a full release session

Important note:
The product should not assume that no app usage automatically means a good day.

### 3. Recovery Score
Measures emotional shift within a session.

**Example:**
- before session: 4/5 angry
- after session: 2/5 irritated
- recovery delta: -2

This can be aggregated weekly or monthly to show emotional regulation improvement over time.

### 4. Reflection Score
Rewards thoughtful completion of the post-release reflection.

This can be based on:
- completing the reflection step
- answering multiple prompts
- returning to review past patterns

This reinforces the app’s core differentiator: emotional awareness, not only release.

### 5. Milestones and Badges
Badges should feel meaningful, warm, and slightly playful.

Examples:
- First Reset
- 3 Calm Days
- 7-Day Awareness
- Paused Before Reacting
- Quiet Win
- Soft Power
- Recovered Faster This Week

### 6. Progress Summaries
Small progress summaries can motivate return visits.

Examples:
- “You checked in 5 days this week.”
- “Your recovery time is improving.”
- “You needed fewer intense sessions this week.”

## Recommended MVP Approach
### Primary Streak
Use **Awareness Streak** as the primary visible streak.

Reason:
- clear and easy to understand
- rewards the desired behavior
- safer than trying to infer emotional success too early

### Secondary Indicators
Include:
- calm days
- weekly emotional average
- number of completed reset sessions
- recovery trend

## Things to Avoid
### 1. Rewarding High Anger Intensity
Do not give more points just because the user logs a more intense emotional state.

### 2. Punishing Broken Streaks Harshly
Avoid shame-based messages such as:
- “You failed.”
- “You lost your control streak.”

Instead, use a gentle reset tone.

### 3. Making the App Feel Competitive
The goal is emotional growth, not emotional leaderboard behavior.

### 4. Treating Emotion Like a Productivity Metric
The product should help users understand themselves, not optimize themselves harshly.

## Suggested Reward Design
### Reward Types
- visual celebration
- badge unlocks
- weekly insight cards
- calm animations
- gentle encouraging copy

### Reward Tone Examples
- “You showed up for yourself today.”
- “That pause mattered.”
- “Not every win is loud.”
- “You handled that better than last time.”

## Future Expansion Ideas
- emotional level-up system based on consistency and recovery
- unlockable themes or visual scenes
- custom rituals or favorite tools
- weekly reflection reports
- personalized growth summaries

## Success Criteria for Gamification
The gamification system is working if users:
- return without feeling pressured
- check in even on calm days
- complete reflections more often
- understand their progress over time
- feel better supported rather than judged
