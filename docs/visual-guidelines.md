# Emotional Reset App — Visual Guidelines

## Visual Goal
The visual system should communicate emotional energy, safety, and progress.

The product should feel:
- emotionally expressive
- slightly moody
- modern and premium
- playful without becoming silly
- calm after the storm

## Visual Personality
Keywords:
- playful
- symbolic
- controlled chaos
- emotional clarity
- premium digital wellness
- soft tension
- recovery

## Tone to Avoid
Avoid visuals that feel:
- violent or graphic
- childish in a toy-like way
- overly clinical
- overly dark or depressing
- comedy-first at the expense of emotional credibility

## Color System
### Base Colors
Use a neutral emotional canvas:
- off-black
- warm dark gray
- soft navy
- muted cream

### Emotional Accent Colors
Map accents to emotional states:
- Calm → muted blue / teal
- Irritated → amber
- Frustrated → orange
- Angry → orange-red
- Explosive → coral-red / magenta-red

### Recovery Colors
Use recovery accents after reflection or completion:
- mint
- pale green
- warm light gold
- soft cream glow

## Shape Language
Use:
- rounded corners
- soft geometry
- fluid or organic motion
- gentle asymmetry

Avoid:
- sharp aggressive forms
- overly rigid systems that feel cold

## Illustration Direction
Illustration should be symbolic rather than literal.

Recommended subjects:
- abstract emotional energy
- stylized tools
- symbolic cracks, particles, or fragments
- visual transitions from tension to calm
- simple atmospheric scenes

Avoid:
- realistic violent destruction
- literal human suffering
- graphic images of broken objects everywhere

## Motion Guidelines
### During Release Interaction
Motion can include:
- shake
- bounce
- crack
- burst
- impact ripple
- drag resistance

### After Release
Motion should visibly settle down:
- particles fade
- scene softens
- ambient light becomes warmer
- visual noise reduces

This transition is important because it tells the story of regulation.

## UI Mood States
### Before Release
- more tension in background
- stronger accent color
- more contrast or subtle vibration

### During Release
- responsive motion
- satisfying impact feedback
- controlled intensity

### After Release
- softer background
- reduced contrast
- warmer or calmer lighting cues
- cleaner visual composition

## Typography Direction
Typography should feel clear, warm, and mature.

Recommended qualities:
- highly readable
- modern sans serif
- medium softness in shape
- enough weight contrast for emotion-driven UI

Avoid fonts that feel:
- too playful and childish
- too aggressive and condensed
- too corporate and sterile

## Asset Strategy
### Good Uses for AI-Generated Assets
Use Midjourney or other image generation tools for:
- mood scenes
- badge concepts
- abstract backgrounds
- symbolic object exploration
- style boards

### Do Not Rely on AI-Generated Assets For
- full UI consistency
- state-based icons that need exact repeatability
- design system assets that require precise reuse

## Midjourney Prompt Direction
Use consistent language across prompts, such as:
- playful premium illustration
- symbolic emotional release
- controlled chaos
- modern digital wellness app
- no text
- no people

## Example Prompt Themes
- emotional release room
- symbolic stress relief tools
- post-release calm scene
- abstract mood cards
- achievement badge concepts

## Content Tone Alignment
Visuals and microcopy should work together.

Examples:
- intense visuals should be balanced by grounded copy
- calm completion screens should feel reassuring, not triumphant in a loud way
- badges should feel warm and affirming, not boastful

## MVP Visual Recommendation
For version 1:
- keep the overall system simple
- use one strong visual direction consistently
- focus on 1–2 release interactions
- build a small but cohesive asset set

Consistency will matter more than volume.
